Part 1

NRC School Newsletter
Special Edition: Birthday Boy Interview
Trey

Happy Birthday

Trey: Oh, Yuu. Did you come all this way to celebrate? Thank you.

Since it’s your birthday, how do you feel today?

T: It’s the same as usual. I told them that they didn’t have to throw a huge party like this for the third year in a row, and yet…

T: Once again, it became quite the lively birthday party.

T: I’m sure there are some who only see this as an opportunity to mess around

T: But well, I’m still grateful that they came by to celebrate regardless. I’ll try to have fun.

Is there anything in particular that you would like?

T: Are you getting something for me? In that case…

T: I want 10,000 Madol per 100 grams worth of high-grade vanilla beans.

S-so expensive…
I will think it over…

T: Hahaha. I was just kidding.

T: I was asked the same question by the rest of the dorm about a month ago.

T: I answered them like I did just now but… I didn’t expect them to take it so seriously.

How exactly did you answer them?

T: For Cater, I told him to “Make the Unbirthday cakes in my place for half a year!”

T: For Deuce, I asked him to “Grow the ingredients I need for making sweets in the botanical gardens for me.”

T: As for Ace, “Help me to maintain my broom for Flying classes.” And finally for Riddle, I said that “I want concise revision notes for my next test.”

T: …I pretty much answered them like that.

How did everyone respond?

T: Some protested, others were shocked, but eventually everyone simply nodded and said they “Got it”.

T: I didn’t think they would take what I said to them so seriously.

In other words… Everything you told them was simply made in jest?

T: Well, of course. Why would it not be? I did tell them I was just joking, afterall.

Part 2

Aside from making sweets, are you good at cooking meals as well?

T: Yeah, during times when my parents were busy tending to the cake shop, I would often be in charge of making dinner.

T: I would try making a variety of different dishes so that we wouldn’t grow bored of eating the same few meals everyday. Thanks to that, I can pretty much cook anything I want.

What would you say is your signature dish?

T: Hm… It would probably be hamburg steak. My younger siblings would ask for it a lot, so I’d end up making it pretty regularly.

T: My hamburg steak is pretty delicious, if I do say so myself.

T: To bring out the best flavour, you can’t beat using Walrus brand oyster sauce as a secret ingredient, and…

…You know you can’t fool me with that lie anymore, right?

T: Haha! Sure, it may certainly have been a joke when we were making mont blanc, but it’s different this time.

T: Oyster sauce is well known for being a seasoning to give meat dishes a richer flavour.

T: Plus, adding an egg, sunny side up with a runny yolk on top is a Clover family specialty. If you mix the piping hot gravy together with the yolk…

T: …Now, try to imagine it.

Gulp…

T: …You look like you’re starving. Okay, okay. I’ll make some for you too next time.

By the way, have you ever failed at cooking?

T: Of course I have. While I may have some talent at making sweets, I’m relatively bad in other areas of cooking.

T: But thankfully for me, even if the end product comes out inedible, the rest of the dorm will still eat it without complaint.

But, why?

T: Fufu… Have you forgotten what my Unique Magic can do?

Wait, don’t tell me, you used ‘Doodle Suit’ to change the flavour?

T: I’m kidding, of course I wouldn’t do something like that… most of the time.

Part 3

What is your favourite food?

T: It’s Candied Violets.

How did you find out that you liked them?

T: If I remember correctly, the first time I tried them was before I entered elementary school.

T: I probably grew so attached to them because I was impressed that edible flowers existed.

T: From that point onwards, I would try to eat any wild flowers I could find growing by the roadside. I can only imagine the amount of trouble I must have caused my parents.

T: Although I personally can’t recall all that much…

The Candied Violets must have been that good.

T: Definitely. I liked the flavour a lot, but that’s not all…

T: When I ate them, it would feel like I was lying in a field of flowers. I also really enjoyed how the floral taste of the violets would spread throughout my mouth.

T: I would get so lost in their flavour, there were times where I would overeat because I’d lose track of how many I had eaten.

T: Especially since my family stocked a lot of Candied Violets to use as cake ingredients,

T: I would get scolded by my parents constantly because I’d often eat them between meals.

T: However, despite all that, I never stopped eating them. So in the end, they’d set aside some Candied Violets for me to eat, if I recall correctly.

T: Even now, my parents still occasionally send some over to me, so I often snack on them when taking a break from studying.

T: By the way, did you know? There are other ways that Candied Violets can be used apart from as a snack. I’d personally recommend adding them to hot milk in place of sugar.

T: If you do, the colour of the violets will mix in with the milk, turning its colour to a light blue. Isn’t that interesting?

Is there any food that you dislike, then?

T: Ah… that’d be Mustard.

T: When we were little, to test our luck, Che’nya and I would set things up so that out of five cream puffs, one would be filled with mustard. Then, we would eat one of the five puffs.

T: I’m sure you can imagine how things turned out. Of course, I ended up picking the one with mustard.

T: There wasn’t even any water nearby, and Che’nya just laughed out loud…

T: I was pretty much at the point of tears by the end of the whole ordeal.

T: But hey, let’s not talk about such bitter memories anymore, since it’s my birthday and all.

Thank you very much for letting me listen to your stories.

Once again, I wish you a happy birthday.
