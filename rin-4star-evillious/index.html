Notes

  1. Whenever Riliane is performing her duties/acting in official capacity, she uses archaic speech patterns (notably, using じゃ instead of だ often, and お主 instead of 君 or 貴方), and the personal pronoun わらわ (warawa), which is supposedly used by females as a humble way to refer to themselves. Like the Evillious translations for the light novels, I chose to use the Royal we to denote these instances. She uses 私 (watashi) when she drops the formalities.　(Return to text)

  1. Leonhart was stated to have rejected the elevation of status to noble after his heroics, so he technically only is: 1. A member of the celebrated Three Heroes and 2. Captain of the Royal Guard. Riliane uses 一介の貴族 (lit. a mere noble) to refer to him and it felt kinda “huh” to translate that exactly so I took some liberties with this.　(Return to text)

  1. Allen always refers to Riliane when they are perfoming their duties as リリアンヌ様 (Riliane-sama), so I used the term of address for princesses (according to yaboi Google, it’s Your Royal Highness) for these instead. He also uses more polite honorifics and 私 instead of 僕 while speaking to her in official capacity.　(Return to text)

  1. Chartette is a maid, although her title is stated as chamberlain here.　(Return to text)

  1. She calls him Kyle-niisama, and no, they aren’t blood-related.　(Return to text)

  1. The Sister in question here is actually Clarith, who was close to Michaela, the “daughter of green” whom Kyle fell for, and Riliane became enraged with jealousy over.　(Return to text)
